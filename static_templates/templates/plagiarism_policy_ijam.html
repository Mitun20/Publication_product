Plagiarism Policy - JME

  • A plagiarized research publication will be rejected by JME, as copying someone’s work without citation amounts to an academic crime and will be strictly dealt with by JME.
  • There may be penalty of rejection of the research publication or the entire research project, in such cases.
  • Hence, all scholars are strongly advised to get well- acquainted with all aspects of plagiarism including definition, inclusions, software used to detect plagiarism etc.
  • It will be the responsibility of the research scholar to check the extent of plagiarism in his/her research publication, synopsis or thesis, before submitting the same to JME.
  • Several, free online software are available on Internet for this purpose.
